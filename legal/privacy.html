01Who We Are

AgentGuard is a cloud-hosted API platform for AI agent security, providing runtime policy enforcement, audit logging, and deployment validation. The Service is operated by The Bot Club (trading as AgentGuard, incorporation pending), based in Australia.

For the purposes of data protection law, The Bot Club / AgentGuard is the data controller for information collected from you as a user of the Service. Where you route data through the AgentGuard API, AgentGuard acts as a data processor on your behalf.

Where GDPR applies, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Contract — processing necessary to provide the Service under our Terms of Service.
  • Legitimate interests — security monitoring, fraud/abuse prevention, product analytics, and service improvement — where our interests are not overridden by your rights.
  • Legal obligation — where processing is required by applicable law.
  • Consent — for any processing not covered above; you may withdraw consent at any time.

03What We Don't Collect

✅ We are deliberately minimal. Our architecture is designed so that the content flowing through your agents never needs to touch our servers.

We do not collect or store:

  • Agent conversation content — the text of messages, prompts, or responses in your AI agent conversations.
  • Tool call parameter values — the actual data values passed to tools (e.g., file contents, query strings, search terms). We log parameter names and types, not values, unless you explicitly configure value-level logging in your policy.
  • End-user PII processed by your agents — names, addresses, financial data, or any other personal information belonging to the users of your applications. You are the controller of that data; we do not see it.
  • Sensitive personal data — health records, biometrics, religious or political beliefs, sexual orientation, or other special-category data as defined under GDPR Article 9.
  • Payment card details — payment processing is handled entirely by our payment provider; we never see your raw card data.

04How We Use Your Data

4.1 Service Operation

We use account and API metadata to authenticate requests, enforce rate limits, generate invoices, and manage your account. Audit log data is stored and indexed to provide you with searchable, exportable compliance records.

4.2 Security Enforcement

IP addresses, request metadata, and usage patterns are analysed to detect abuse, protect the platform from attack, and enforce our Acceptable Use Policy. We may temporarily block IPs or API keys exhibiting anomalous behaviour.

4.3 Usage Analytics

Aggregated, anonymised usage data (e.g., total evaluations per policy type, popular compliance templates, median latency distributions) is used to understand how the Service is used and to prioritise product improvements. This data does not identify individual users.

We do not send marketing emails to free-tier users without explicit opt-in. You may unsubscribe from non-essential communications at any time via the link in any email or by contacting us.

4.5 Legal Compliance

We may use and disclose data as required by applicable law, court order, or government authority — or to protect the rights, property, or safety of AgentGuard, our users, or the public.

05Data Storage & Infrastructure

5.1 Where Your Data Lives

All customer data is stored in Azure — Australia East region (Sydney datacentre). We do not replicate data outside of Australia by default.

5.2 Database

Primary storage uses PostgreSQL, running on Azure-managed infrastructure with encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+).

5.3 Audit Log Integrity

Audit logs are protected by a cryptographic hash chain: each log entry includes a hash of the previous entry, making retrospective modification detectable. We do not provide a mechanism to delete individual audit log entries, as doing so would break chain integrity.

5.4 Security Measures

We implement industry-standard security controls including:

  • Encryption at rest and in transit for all customer data.
  • Role-based access controls and principle of least privilege for internal access.
  • API key hashing — we store a hash of your key, not the key itself.
  • Regular security reviews and dependency updates.
  • Cloudflare DDoS protection and WAF in front of the API.

Audit Log Retention & GDPR

Because audit logs are maintained as a hash-chained integrity record, individual entries cannot be deleted without breaking the chain. If you have a GDPR erasure request that would require audit log deletion, contact us — we can discuss options including account-level chain termination and the implications for audit integrity.

Audit logs contain only structural metadata (tool names, decision outcomes, timestamps) — not personal data in most configurations. If you have configured your policies to log parameter values that may include personal data, you are responsible for ensuring that configuration complies with your own data protection obligations.

08Cookies & Tracking

✅ No cookies. AgentGuard is primarily an API service. We do not set cookies on your browser.

The AgentGuard API does not use cookies. Our static dashboard (developer console) uses only browser localStorage to store your API key locally in your browser — this never leaves your device and is not accessible to us.

We do not use:

  • Session cookies or persistent cookies.
  • Third-party tracking pixels or scripts.
  • Fingerprinting technologies.
  • Any form of cross-site tracking.

Cloudflare, as our CDN/DNS provider, may set a short-lived session cookie (__cf_bm) for bot management purposes. This is a security measure, not a tracking cookie, and is subject to Cloudflare's Privacy Policy.
