⏱ Compliance Deadline

EU AI Act obligations for high-risk AI systems are actively enforced. GPAI model provisions entered force in August 2025. August 2026 is the hard deadline for full technical compliance documentation for high-risk AI systems — including agentic AI deployed in regulated sectors. The evidence trail needs to exist before the deadline, not during the audit.

Are Your Agents In Scope?

The first question GRC teams ask is: "Does the EU AI Act apply to us?" The answer, if you're deploying AI agents in or affecting EU citizens, is almost certainly yes — and the relevant provisions are broader than most compliance teams initially assume.

The EU AI Act applies to any AI system that is:

  • Placed on the EU market — even if you're a US or UK company, if EU users interact with your AI system, the Act applies
  • Used in the EU — including internal enterprise deployments where EU employees interact with AI agents
  • Deployed as a "high-risk AI system" — determined by the sector and use case, not the technology type

What Makes an AI Agent "High-Risk"?

The EU AI Act's Annex III defines high-risk AI systems by sector and use case. AI agents are most likely to be classified as high-risk when they operate in:

Sector High-Risk Agent Use Cases
Financial Services Credit assessment, fraud detection, trading, customer onboarding, KYC/AML
Healthcare Clinical decision support, patient triage, medical record analysis, drug interactions
HR & Employment CV screening, candidate ranking, employee monitoring, performance evaluation
Law Enforcement Risk assessment, predictive tools, evidence analysis
Critical Infrastructure Energy grid management, water systems, transport operations
Education Student assessment, admission decisions, learning path determination
Public Services Benefits eligibility
